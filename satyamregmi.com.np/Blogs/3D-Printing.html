3D Printing


1. What is 3D printing?
3D printing is the process of manufacturing three-dimensional objects using additive manufacturing methods. By extruding layers of plastic or metal material, these machines reproduce real world solid models from digital designs created with computer aided design programs or CAD software. Using these printers, designers can make physical prototypes or production-quality parts without investing thousands of dollars in tooling or hiring skilled engineers.

2. How do I use the printer?
There are two different types of 3D printers: desktop and industrial. Desktop printers are usually smaller than an office desk and have minimal moving parts. Industrial printers are larger versions of desktop units that fit inside a factory. Industrial printers are capable of producing high precision, complex components while being able to withstand harsh environments and produce parts at massive volumes. There are many advantages to 3D printing, including the possibility of creating custom products, building in almost any scale, designing and sculpting directly in their final product, and having a finished product ready in hours instead of weeks.

3. Is there a cost associated with 3D printing?
The initial investment to purchase a 3D printer is expensive (starting around $3k), but once purchased, they tend to last longer than traditional manufacturing methods. In addition, the price per unit decreases over time. If printed items aren't profitable, manufacturers may not continue printing them. Once a company decides to print something, however, there's no turning back - if the item sells, then the manufacturer will likely expand its line of goods to print more.

4. Where can I get started with 3D printing?
Many people start out using desktop 3D printers, though you can find industrial printers online and even in hobby shops. To create a model on a desktop printer, you'll need to download a free application that creates a virtual representation of the object you want to print and then upload the file onto the machine. After the file is uploaded, the 3D printer starts to build the design layer by layer until it is complete. Desktop printers can take anywhere from 30 minutes to 4 hours to print depending on the complexity of the project.

5. Can you print anything?
Yes! You can print just about anything that is possible to convert into a digital format. If you're interested in some projects that others have completed, check out the Thingiverse website.
